About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate in Radiology at the University of Pittsburgh interested in developing imaging biomarkers for mammographically-occult cancers in women with dense breasts using screening Digital Breast Tomosynthesis.

I earned my PhD in Biomedical Engineering from The University of Texas at Austin, where my work advanced the quality of life for breast cancer patients and survivors, and applied generative AI to enhance diagnostic workflows in skin cancer care.

My research interests include medical imaging, machine learning, and quantitative and qualitative studies.

In my personal life, I enjoy movies, video games, yoga, scuba diving, and traveling.
