Medical Image Analysis and Predictive Modeling

I analyzed 3D body images, demographics, and medical records of breast reconstruction patients to investigate pre- and post-operative body measurement changes [1-3]. Using parametric and non-parametric hypothesis tests, such as paired t-tests and Wilcoxon signed-rank tests, along with regression models (e.g., Mixed-effects models, General Estimating Equations, and Robust Regression), I predicted post-operative changes based on pre-operative measurements, age, and BMI. Additionally, I assessed changes in garment-related outcomes using validated patient-reported measures (e.g., BREAST-Q, ASI-R, and BIS). My findings revealed significant improvements in garment-related outcomes post-reconstruction, particularly among patients undergoing autologous reconstruction. This research underscores my proficiency in quantitative image analysis and statistical modeling.

Quantitative and Qualitative Studies for Personalized Healthcare Solutions

I conducted surveys, focus groups, and interviews to identify the garment needs in breast cancer care from both patients/survivors and providers, utilizing thematic analysis to develop valuable insights. These efforts informed the development of personalized recovery garments, incorporating feedback from both patients and physician assistants at MD Anderson. This work exemplifies my ability to integrate diverse perspectives into practical healthcare innovations.

Generative AI for Medical Applications

I developed a generative adversarial network (GAN)-based model to transform Reflectance Confocal Microscopy (RCM) images into Hematoxylin and Eosin (H&E) stained images, effectively simulating targeted histological stains [8]. This innovation demonstrated the potential of generative AI to enhance diagnostic workflows by mimicking traditional staining techniques. The model outperformed existing methods in terms of efficiency and accuracy, showcasing its applicability in medical imaging and pathology. My expertise in generative AI demonstrates the potential of deep learning to optimize diagnostic workflows and showcases my ability to drive advancements in medical imaging.

Reporting Student Demographics in Biomedical Engineering Education

We examined student demographic data collection, reporting, and stratification in papers published in Biomedical Engineering Education to provide recommendations to improve the clarity and consistency of future papers in the journal. Of 150 papers we reviewes, 132 reported on student populations, with most being undergraduates. Of 132 papers with student populations, only 39 included demographic data, most often gender/sex and race/ethnicity. Reporting demographics has increased over time but often lacked detail on methods, and stratification was rare. We provide six recommendations to improve clarity, consistency, and equity in demographic reporting.
